Sub-Category: Poems

Type: Aliyyot Poems

When Recited: Parashat Yitro and Parashat Vaetanan

Composer: N/A (String of Biblical Verses)

 

 

 

Introduction:

This poem is recited after the aliyya in which we read the 10 commandments. The 10 commandments begin with the Hebrew word (Anochi) (Exodus 20:2). This collection of verses also contains the word (Anochi) or alternatively (Ani) in each verse. We have colored this word in red for you in each verse.

 

 

Anochi anochi adonai veen mibbaladai moshiya.

I, even I, am Adonai; and beside me there is none that rescues. (Isaiah 43:11)

Anochi higgadti vehoshati vehishmati veen bachem zar, veattem edai neum adonai vaani el.

I have declared, and I have rescued, and I have announced, and there was no strange god among you; therefore you are my witnesses, says Adonai, and I am God. (Isaiah 43:12)

Attem edai neum adonai veavdi asher baarti, lemaan tedeu vetavinu ki ani hu lefanai lo notsar el veaarai lo yihy.

You are my witnesses, says Adonai, and my servant whom I have chosen; that you may know and believe me, and understand that I am he; before me there was no god formed, neither shall any be after me. (Isaiah 43:10)

Veanochi adonai elohecha meerets mitsrayim, od oshivecha vaohalim kim moed.

But I am Adonai your God from the land of Egypt; I will yet again settle you in tents, as in the days of the appointed season. (Hosea 10:12)

Gam miyom ani hu veen miyadi mats-tsil, efal umi yeshivenna.

Yea, since day existed, I have been he, and there is none that can deliver out of my hand; I will act, and who can reverse it? (Isaiah 43:13)

Mi faal veasa or haddorot merosh, ani adonai rishon veet aaronim ani hu.

Who has acted and done it? He that called the generations from the beginning. I, Adonai, who am the first, and with the last I am the same. (Isaiah 41:4)

Veanochi adonai elohecha meerets mitsrayim, velohim zulati lo teda umoshiya ayin bilti.

Yet I am the Adonai your God since the Land of Egypt; and you know no god but me, and beside me there is no saviour. (Hosea 13:4)

Anochi adonai elohecha hammaalecha meerets mitsrayim, harev-picha vaamalehu

I am Adonai your God, who brought you up out of the Land of Egypt; open your mouth wide, and I will fill it. (Psalms 81:11)
