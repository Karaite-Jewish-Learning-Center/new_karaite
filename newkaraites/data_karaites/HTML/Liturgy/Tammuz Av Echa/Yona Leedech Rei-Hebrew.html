יוֹנָה, לְאֵידֵךְ רְאִי / קוֹלֵךְ בְּצֶוַח שְׂאִי

כִּי בָא בְאַרְצֵךְ שְׁאִי / יָגוֹן בְּגִילָיִךְ:

 

Yona, le’edech re’i / ḳolech betsevaḥ se’i

Ki va ve’artsech she’i / yaḡon beḡilayich.

 

O dove, see, and raise your voice in cry,

For destruction has befallen your land; and anguish, your joy.

י֯וֹנַת אֲהָבִים, בְּנִי / קִנֵּךְ בְּמִדְבָּר, וְנִי

וָהִי כְּשִׁירִים עֲנִי / מָרָה בְּקוֹלָיִךְ:

Yonat ahavim beni / ḳinnech bemidbar veni

Vahi keshirim ‘ani / mara beḳolayich.

 

O beloved dove, build your nest in the wilderness, and moans

And wails you should call out, like songs, bitterly, with your voice.

וּ֯בְהַר חֳרָבוֹת עֲלִי / תַּנִּים נְהִי שַׁאֲלִי

עִמָּם סְפֹדִי, בְּלִי / דוּמָם לְחֵלָיִךְ:

 

Uv-har ḥoravot ‘ali / tannim nehi sha’ali

‘Immam sefodi beli / dumam leḥelayich.

 

And ascend the mount of destruction, borrow the moan of jackals,

With them, mourn, giving no silence to your strength.

סִ֯פְקִי בְּיָדֵךְ, וְאִי / קִרְאִי בְּעֶצֶב בְּאִי

מֶרְחָק כְּנֶשֶׁר דְּאִי / בִּימֵי שְׁכוּלָיִךְ:

 

Sifḳi beyadech ve’i / ḳiri be‘etsev be’i

Merḥaḳ kenesher de’i / bimé shechulayich.

 

Clap your hands [in anguish], and oh, alas, / call out with sadness, with “Oh, alas!”

Fly to a distance, like an eagle, in the days of your bereavement.

פִּ֯תְחִי סְגוֹרֵי לְבִיב / מִלְאִי יְגוֹנֵי שְׁבִיב

בִּעֵר בְּקֹדֶשׁ, סְבִיב / מִזְבַּח כְּלִילָיִךְ:

 

Pitḥi seḡoré leviv / mili yeḡoné sheviv

Bi‘er beḳodesh seviv / mizbaḥ kelilayich.

 

Open your heart’s enclosure, fill up with anguish about the flame

That burned in the Temple, around your crowning altar.

בָּ֯דָד לְאֶרֶץ שְׁבִי / דִּמְעָה לְעֵינֵךְ רְוִי

כִּבְאֵר לְמֵי שַׁאֲבִי / יָבְשׁוּ נְחָלָיִךְ:

 

Badad le’erets shevi / dim‘a le‘enech revi

Kiver lemé sha’avi / yaveshu neḥalayich.

 

Sit alone on the ground, and overflow your eye with tears,

Like a well, draw [their] water, [until] your rivers are dry.

נִ֯דָּה כְּסִיס רַחֲקִי / תֹּעֶה כְּשִׁכּוֹר בְּקִי

וַחְנִי יְעֵנִים בְּקִי־ / נָה הִי מְחוֹלָיִךְ:

 

Nidda kesis raḥaḳi / to‘é keshikkor beḳi

Vaḥni ye‘enim beḳi- / na hi meḥolayich.

 

Stay apart, withdraw like a crane, as you wander in vomit like a drunkard,

And camp with ostriches in lament and sighs, [instead of] your dances.

מָרָה בְּבִכְיָה בְעִי / רַבּוּ חֲלָלָיִךְ:

Call out in cries, bitterly. Many are your people that have been killed!

שֹׁ֯מִּי עֲדִינָה בְּזֶה / מָשָׁל לְנָדִיב בְּזֶה

Ḥodesh leshana hazé / hirbu ḥalalayich.

 

Be appalled about this, O you who are given to luxury; you are compared to a noble. On this

Month of the year, they killed so many of your people.

הַ֯כִּי לְחָיֵךְ, עֲלֵי / דָמִים, דְּבִירֵךְ מְלֵא

לוֹטָן בְּתוֹכוֹ שְׁלֵה / שִׁבֵּר נְעוּלָיִךְ:

Lotan betocho shelé / shibber ne‘ulayich.

 

Slap your cheeks, about the blood [that has been shed], with your Temple full [of tears or blood];

Lotan, sitting at ease inside it, had broken your locks [and gained entrance].

Uncovering the shield, and the outpour of the wickedness of your great ones.

זַ֯כּוּ נְזִירֵךְ כְּמוֹ / שֶׁלֶג לְבָנוֹן שְׁמוֹ

חָשַׁךְ מְאוֹרָם, כְּמוֹ / תֹּפְשֵׂי חֲלִילָיִךְ:

Ḥashach me’oram kemo / tofesé ḥalilayich.

 

Your nazirites were pure like the snow called Lebanon.

Their brightness has now become dark, like those that hold your flutes.

בַּשֵּׂר אֲמָתְךָ תְּמוֹל / לִבְשִׁי מְעִילָיִךְ:

Call out to your Rock, to have mercy: “Treat me with true kindness.

Announce, to your maidservant, already:  Wear your raiments!”

כַּכָּתוּב: עוּרִ֥י עוּרִ֛י לִבְשִׁ֥י עֻזֵּ֖ךְ צִיּ֑וֹן לִבְשִׁי בִּגְדֵי תִפְאַרְתֵּךְ:

 

Kakkatuv: ‘Uri ‘uri livshi ‘uzzech tsiyyon, livshi biḡdé tifartech.

 

As it is written: Awake, awake, Put on thy strength, O Zion; Put on thy beautiful garments, O Jerusalem, the holy city.
