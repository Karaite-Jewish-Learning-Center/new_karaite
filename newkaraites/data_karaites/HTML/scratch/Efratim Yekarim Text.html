אֶ֒פְרָתִים יְקָרִים
נִלְכְּדוּ מֵעֲבָרִים
יוֹם שָׁאֲגוּ כְפִירִים
מֵהַֽרְרֵי נְמֵרִים:

Efratim yeḳarim
Nilkedu me‘avarim
Yom sha’aḡu chefirim
Meharerē nemerim.

The dear noble people
Were captured from all sides,
On the day when the lions roared
From the mountains of tigers.

הֶ֒מְיַת בְּנֵי שִׁמְעוֹן
וְלֵוִי וְכָל בְּנֵי עוֹנְ־
נָה עָלְתָה לִמְעוֹן
רֹאשׁ כָּל הֶהָרִים.

Hemyat benē shim‘on,
Velevi vechol benē one-
Na ‘aleta lim‘on
Rosh kol heharim.

The loud cry of the children of Simeon,
And Levi, and all the children of sorcerors,
Has ascended to the heavenly lair,
High above all mountains.

רָ֒חֵל מְיַלֶּלֶת
וְלֵאָה מְגֹלֶלֶת
בְּלַהַב וְגַחֶלֶת
וְקֶרַח מִמְּזָרִים:

Raḥel meyallelet
Vele’a meḡolelet
Belahav veḡaḥelet
Veḳeraḥ mimmezarim.

Rachel cries out,
And Leah is rolling around
In flames and embers
And ice from the stormy heavens.

נָ֒עוּ מַעְגְּלוֹתַי
וְשָׁמְמוּ מִזְבְּחוֹתַי

Veshamemu mizbeḥotai
Veshachenu ve’armenotai
Mo’av vehaḡrim.

My paths went off,

And my altars were laid desolate.

And in my palaces dwelled

Moab and Hagrites.

חָ֒דְלוּ בְּיִשְׂרָאֵל
הֵיכָל וְהַהַרְאֵל
וְאֵין דּוֹרֵשׁ וְשֹׁאֵל
בְּמִשְׁפַּט הָאֻרִים:

Ḥadelu beyisra’el
Hechal vehahar’el
Ve’en doresh vesho’el
Bemishpat ha’urim.

Gone from Israel

Are the Temple and the altar,

And there is no [priest] that consults and asks

The verdict of the Urim.

זֹ֒הַר דְּבִיר וְאוּלָם
עַמִּי וְכָל חֵילָם
הֵן אֹיְבִי כִלָּם
זְקֵנִים עִם נְעָרִים:

Zohar devir ve’ulam
‘Ammi vechol ḥelam
Hen oyevi chillam
Zeḳenim ‘im ne‘arim.


The gleaming Holy of Holies and Temple Hall,

[And] my people and all their forces —

My enemy has finished them all off,

Old and young.

קָ֒רַב שְׁאוֹנִי וְאָרַב

Ari yoshev bema’arav
Ḥatserim bemistarim.

The noise against me came near, and ambushed

My Temple, and destroyed it —

The lion, waiting in ambush

Hidden in the courtyards.

וַ֒יּוֹר חֵץ קְלוֹקֵל

He shot a swift arrow

Into my people’s heart, and he perverted

His ways; outside the house, the sword

Bereaved us, and inside.

The cypress and box tree went forlorn,
And the moon’s light went dark,
When the [Temple] Mount was despoiled,
Which the rivers plundered.

Then there was war at the gates.

צ֒וּר תְּקַבֵּץ וְתִשְׁמֹר

Tsur teḳabbets vetishmor
Yeshurun behar hammor
Yerannen lach bemizmor
Ḥadashim labbeḳarim.

O Rock, gather and preserve

Jeshurun [=the Jewish people] on Mt. Mor [Moriah, the Temple Mout].

They will sing to you a psalm,

Newly, every morning.
